The core principle

Filling a bank shift still costs money. You save only the premium (agency price minus bank price), not the whole shift.

cash = agency spend × displacement × premium / (1 + premium)

What the numbers rest on

  • Pay: 2026/27 NHS AfC midpoints, 1,957.5 h/yr, 8h shifts, 20% on-cost
  • Agency premium: ~20% (House of Commons Library), contested, editable
  • Displacement stance: Conservative ~13% (default), Pilot ~26%, Optimistic ~35%
  • Platform cost: £17k/yr, the ROI denominator
  • Pilot evidence: 2-site data in a 4-trust programme; indicative, not solely attributable

Two modes, by audience

Quick estimate: three inputs (bank pool, agency fill, roster team). For operational managers and events.

Detailed / commercial: agency spend by staff group, editable premium and levers. For finance leads and workforce directors.

Configurable & personalised

Live, no code: pick an organisation type, set its size (total bank headcount and agency spend), then refine per staff group, premium, stance and optional levers. Everything recomputes instantly and is most credible on the trust's own rates.

In code (owner): one central constants block to re-base pay each year, rebrand, re-localise (en-GB, AU-ready), edit the org templates, and wire up lead capture.

Why it is built this way

Defensible over impressive: conservative defaults, a transparent formula, every assumption shown with its source, and honest caveats. The same tool scales from a 30-second event estimate to a finance-grade business case.

Strategic fit: releasing cash and capacity from temporary staffing supports the NHS 10 Year Plan and Workforce Paper, and the Staff Health & Wellbeing CQUIN.  ·  Figures are indicative, conservative and editable.  ·  Two formats (web embed + touchscreen kiosk) share one calculation engine, so they always agree.
